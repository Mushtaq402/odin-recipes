Chicken Biryani

Description

Chicken Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic Indian/Pakistani dish made with layers of marinated chicken, fragrant basmati rice, and a blend of spices. It is often garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and served with raita or salad.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • Fried onions for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil and sauté the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half of the onions for garnish.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste to the pot and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they are browned on all sides.
  6. Stir in the cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined with the chicken.
  7. Reduce the heat and add the yogurt to the pot. Mix well and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  8. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and add salt. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked, then drain and set aside.
  9. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken in the pot. Cover with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and has absorbed the flavors of the chicken.
  10. Garnish with fried onions and fresh cilantro before serving. Serve hot with raita or salad on the side.

Serving Suggestions

Chicken Biryani is best served hot with a side of raita (yogurt sauce) or a simple salad. It can also be accompanied by papadums or pickle.
