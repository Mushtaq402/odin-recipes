Mutton Rosh

Description

Rosh is also called "Mutton Rosh" or "Namkeen Gosht" is a traditional Pakistani dish from the city of Peshawar and Blochistan. Usually made with mutton or lamb. it's cooked slowly in its own fat and juices, giving it a tender, salty and aromatic flavor. The dish is often enjoyed with naan or roti, and is a beloved comfort food in Pakistani cuisine, especially during colder months.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb or beef (with bones, preferably ribs or shank)
  • 2-3 tbsp desi ghee (or oil)
  • 4-5 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 medium potatoes (optional, cut into halves)
  • 2-3 green chilies (whole)
  • 1 medium onion (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp whole garam masala (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves)
  • Salt (to taste, usually generous)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 4-5 cups water

Steps

  1. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottom pot or clay pot.
  2. Add whole garam masala and sliced onion, sauté lightly.
  3. Add meat pieces and sear until slightly browned.
  4. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and salt.
  5. Add potatoes and green chilies.
  6. Pour in water, cover, and cook on low flame for 1.5-2 hours until meat is tender.
  7. Add lemon juice before serving for a fresh touch.

Serving suggestion

  1. Traditionally served with lassi (yogurt drink), daal, and sometimes French fries on the side.
  2. Best enjoyed with naan or chapati.
