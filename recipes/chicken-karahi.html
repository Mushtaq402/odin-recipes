Chicken Karahi

Description

Chicken Karahi is a famous Pakistani and North Indian dish known for its bold, aromatic flavours and rustic preparation. It is prepared in a pan called Karahi, it features tender chicken pieces simmered in a thick tomato based gravy infused with ginger, garlic and a medley of spices such as cumin, coriander and garam-masalah. The dish is often finished with fresh green chillies, cilantro and a squeeze of lemon juice, giving a vibrant, tangy kick that balances the warmth of the spices. Served hot with naan or roti, Chicken Karahi is both comforting and celebratory, making it a staple at family gatherings and festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (1kg)
  • Tomatoes (4 medium, chopped roughly)
  • Cooking Oil or Desi Ghee (1/2 cup)
  • Garlic paste (1 tablespoon)
  • Ginger paste (1 tablespoon)
  • Salt (to taste, usually 1 teaspoon)
  • Red chilli powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Black pepper (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Crushed coriander seed (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Crushed cumin seed (1/2)
  • Green chillies (2, slit lengthwise)
  • Fresh cilantro (handful chopped)
  • Lemon juice (optional, for tangy finish)

Steps

  1. Heat Oil in a Karahi or deep pan
  2. Add Garlic paste and saute until fragrant.
  3. Add the Chicken pieces and fry on medium-high heat until they turn golden.
  4. Stir in the Ginger paste, then add Salt, Red chilli powder, Black pepper, Coriander seeds, and Cumin seeds. Cook for 10 minutes to coat the chicken with spices
  5. Add chopped Tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices, forming a thick gravy.
  6. Continue cooking on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the oil separates from the masalah (a sign the gravy is ready)
  7. Add Green chillies and simmer for 5-10 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and tender
  8. garnish with Fresh cilantro and a squeeze of Lemon juice before serving.

Serving suggestion

Chicken Karahi is best enjoyed with naan, roti or steamed basmati rice.
